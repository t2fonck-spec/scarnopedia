Ianternet "Opdick" McAnus (username: bigboyeen) is a member of Threat Level Midnight and one of its most philosophically interesting figures. Best known for delivering the binary cheese judgment during the Kraft Singles Incident, Ian subsequently underwent a sincere philosophical transformation that resulted in the server's most oft-quoted life motto: "Take nothing seriously."

Ian is a prolific reactor — his emoji reactions to others' messages are a consistent presence across all channels — and an occasional but potent contributor to text-based discussion. He is also known, separately, for his participation in the APC-21 constitutional debate, where he went by the handle drcocoapuff (or BigBoyEen), and for a creative caper involving a spoilered driver's license image and a threat of identity theft.

Overview

Ian's defining characteristic is an extremely flexible response to the chaos around him. Rather than trying to impose order on TLM's discourse, he absorbed the disorder of The Kraftening and converted it into a governing life philosophy. This makes him, arguably, the server's most adaptive member — able to find equilibrium regardless of the surrounding emotional weather.

Role in The Kraftening

Ianternet McAnus issued the definitive binary judgment of the Kraft Singles Incident:

"craft beer cheese: good / kraft beer cheese: bad"
— Ianternet McAnus, January 21, 2023

This ruling, delivered with the authority of a verdict, attempted to close the debate by establishing clear moral territory. It did not close the debate. It escalated it. But the elegance of Ian's formulation — the symmetrical structure, the absolute moral clarity — remains one of the most formally satisfying statements in TLM history.

The Philosophy of "Take Nothing Seriously"

The aftermath of The Kraftening wrought genuine change upon Ianternet McAnus. In July 2023, he revealed the transformation:

"That incident led me to a perspective change in this server: take nothing seriously."
— Ianternet McAnus, July 10, 2023

This statement is remarkable for several reasons. First, it represents an unusually sincere moment of self-reflection in a server that often prioritizes irony. Second, it reveals that The Kraftening's impact extended beyond the comedic — it genuinely altered a member's approach to online interaction. Third, it is one of the few instances in TLM history where a member explicitly articulated a personal philosophy derived from a server experience.

"Take nothing seriously" has since entered the informal lexicon of TLM as both practical advice and gentle irony.

APC-21 Constitutional Debate

Under the handle drcocoapuff (or BigBoyEen), Ian participated in the Constitutional Debate in APC-21. This debate covered states' rights, federal authority, and the Obergefell v. Hodges ruling. Ian's contributions brought the same sharp binary logic he applied to cheese to the realm of constitutional law — an approach that, whether or not it produced correct conclusions, produced memorable argumentation.

The Driver's License Caper

In what is now considered a classic bit of Conference Room comedy, Ian encountered a spoilered image that Thaddy had shared in the channel. Identifying it as a driver's license, Ian reposted the image and announced the ability to commit identity theft. The exchange that followed touched on driver's license number permanence, the casual nature of personal data sharing among friends, and the ethics of opportunistic identity appropriation.

"Now I can steal your identity."
— Ianternet McAnus, upon viewing the driver's license

This was apparently not acted upon.

Interests and Reactions

Ian is notable for his consistent and high-volume use of emoji reactions throughout TLM channels. The "THIS" and "Agreenis" server emojis are particularly associated with his reaction patterns. He appears to use reactions as a primary mode of communication — a sort of compressed commentary that allows him to respond to dozens of messages per session without extensive typing.

  • Constitutional law: Demonstrated genuine engagement in the APC-21 debate
  • Binary thinking: A preference for clean, symmetrical judgments (good/bad; craft/Kraft)
  • Emoji reactions: A primary communication medium
  • Philosophical reflection: Has demonstrated capacity for genuine personal insight
  • Opportunistic identity theft jokes
