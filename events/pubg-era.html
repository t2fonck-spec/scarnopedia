The PUBG Era

From Scarnopedia, the free encyclopedia. Winner winner, chicken dinner.

The PUBG Era refers to the period in Threat Level Midnight server history — beginning in March 2018 — during which PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds dominated the gaming discussion and served as the primary social activity binding members together. It predates the Kraft Singles Incident by approximately five years and represents an earlier, more innocent time in TLM history — a time when members' most pressing concern was getting a chicken dinner.

Overview

PUBG arrived in early access in 2017 and exploded into mainstream gaming culture in 2018, bringing TLM along with it. The Conference Room became a coordination hub for squad formation, post-game debriefs, and kill count celebrations. The phrase "PUBG BOIS" appears repeatedly in the archives as the standard rallying cry for gathering teammates.

Squad Formation Culture

PUBG's squad mode required coordination that translated naturally to a Discord community. Members would call out availability, form groups, and dive in together. The process built camaraderie in a way that solo gaming could not replicate — every chicken dinner was a shared victory.

"Can't wait to try pubg with you guys."
— A TLM member, on the cusp of the PUBG Era

Notable Moments

The Perfect Squad Survival Game

The most celebrated PUBG achievement documented in TLM archives is a game in which an entire squad — all members — survived through to the final ring. This outcome, which PUBG players understand to be both deeply satisfying and extremely rare, generated substantial celebration in the Conference Room.

"Our entire squad lived the entire game. It was amazing to get to the end with 5 alive."
— A TLM member, after the perfect squad game

Agent Michael Scarn's Kill Record

Agent Michael Scarn is documented as having celebrated games in which he achieved 2–3 kills as significant personal improvements. The community received these updates with warmth — an early indicator of TLM's fundamental supportiveness of member growth.

"I'm not sure how to tell how many kills we got as a squad, but I know it was several."
— A TLM member, on the statistical complexity of PUBG

Legacy

The PUBG Era established patterns that persist in TLM today: the practice of gaming together, the Conference Room as a coordination hub, and a culture of celebrating personal achievement regardless of objective performance level. When other members cheer for a 2-kill game, that's the PUBG Era's lasting gift.
