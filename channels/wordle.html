Wordle is one of Threat Level Midnight's newest channels, established in July 2025. Despite its brief existence, the channel has accumulated 774 messages — 703 of which are automated bot posts, making it perhaps the most bot-dominated channel in TLM history. What remains of the human conversation is a thin but revealing layer of competitive anguish and vocabulary-related confusion.

Overview

The channel functions as a group Wordle competition, with members submitting their daily results and an APP bot tracking scores, streaks, and winners. The human contributors — Thaddy Thee Galleon (18), Goffers (15), King Scubdu (15), Ianternet (9), and Agent Michael Scarn (5) — provide commentary that ranges from strategic analysis to existential despair over five-letter words.

The 136-Day Streak

The channel's crowning achievement is a group streak that reached at least 136 consecutive days — a feat requiring every participating member to solve the puzzle daily without fail. In a server where members routinely forget to check channels for weeks, this level of sustained commitment is nothing short of remarkable.

"Your group is on a 136 day streak!"
— APP bot, delivering the kind of validation that humans crave from machines

The Goffers Dynasty

Goffers has emerged as the channel's most consistent winner, claiming the top spot on numerous days and establishing what can only be described as a Wordle dynasty. His dominance is occasionally interrupted by moments of humility:

"Wtf is an atria"
— Goffers, proving that even champions have vocabulary blind spots

Notable Incidents

  • The Bill Arrival — Agent Michael Scarn joined the Wordle group late and had to ask "How do I play here?" — a question that was met with the patience typically reserved for teaching grandparents how to use smartphones
  • The Double Letter Revelation — King Scubdu's admission that he forgot words could contain duplicate letters sent shockwaves through the group
  • The Marshall Naming Convention — Thaddy Thee Galleon complimented King Scubdu's naming abilities, suggesting that even the act of naming the Wordle group was treated as a creative endeavor
"My dumb ass forgot that you can have double letters"
— King Scubdu, having a revelation that costs him a streak
"How do I play here?"
— Agent Michael Scarn, arriving fashionably late to the word game

Cultural Significance

Wordle represents a fascinating evolution in TLM's channel ecosystem: a space where competition is distilled to its purest form — five letters, six guesses, no excuses. The 91% bot message ratio also raises important philosophical questions about what constitutes a "conversation" in the modern Discord server.
