Minecon (formally ⛏️-minecon) is a gaming channel in Threat Level Midnight dedicated to Minecraft, the block-based survival game that has been culturally inescapable since 2011. The channel's activity pattern — an intense burst in early 2023 followed by over a year of silence, then a revival in mid-2024 — perfectly mirrors the universal Minecraft experience of passionate commitment followed by total abandonment followed by "we should start a new world."

Overview

Minecon was the domain of Ben (bUNGER), who posted 371 of the channel's 952 messages (39%) and served as both server administrator and chief architect. Ben's dominance of the channel was not merely statistical but spiritual — he was the one staying up until 3 AM placing blocks while his fellow members slept, a pattern recognizable to anyone who has ever hosted a Minecraft server and discovered that "multiplayer" mostly means "you build everything while your friends log in once to look around."

The Create Mod Era

The TLM Minecraft server ran modded, with the Create mod serving as the centerpiece of the technical experience. Create, which adds mechanical systems, conveyor belts, and elaborate automation to Minecraft, appealed to TLM's engineering sensibilities and provided a framework for the server's most ambitious projects:

  • Auto farms — Ben's proposal to place an automated farm in the center of the base reflected both practical efficiency and a desire for communal infrastructure
  • Railroads — Marshall advocated for long-distance rail networks over teleportation, arguing that the journey was part of the experience
  • The Blue Skies portal — An interdimensional gateway that presumably led somewhere impressive, though documentation on what was found on the other side is sparse
"Also; are charcoal blocks just not a thing?"
— Ben (bUNGER), confronting the hard limits of Minecraft's crafting system
"well maybe we don't want the tp thing if we are doing create because we can make long railroads"
— Marshall (King Scubdu), choosing immersion over convenience

The Trenton Expedition

Perhaps the channel's most relatable moment came from Trenton, whose 4,000-meter expedition to find a specific tree type ended in the only way such expeditions can:

"i ended up traveling 4000 meters out to look for a specific tree just to realize that it was right in front of my house the whole time. big dumbo brian moment"
— Trenton, discovering the tree was inside him all along (or at least at his front door)

The phrase "big dumbo brian moment" has been preserved in the Scarnopedia archives as a candidate for TLM's most endearing typo, though whether "brian" was intentional remains a matter of scholarly debate.

Activity Pattern

The channel's bifurcated timeline tells a familiar story. The January–March 2023 burst produced the majority of messages, driven by the excitement of a new modded world. Activity then flatlined for fifteen months before reviving in June 2024, presumably after someone said the words "we should play Minecraft again" in a voice channel. The revival persisted through February 2025, suggesting either genuine sustained interest or a particularly elaborate world that took longer to abandon.

Top Contributors

Ben (371) dominated, followed by Thad (262), Marshall (110), Trenton (67), Ian (53), and Bill (22). Bill's 22 messages across two separate Minecraft eras suggest he logged in approximately twice per era, looked around, said something polite, and returned to whatever show he was rewatching.
