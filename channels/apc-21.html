APC-21 (formally 📬-apc-21, Channel ID: 672850403339272192) is the Watercooler Talk channel of Threat Level Midnight. With over 52 MB of archived messages and 172+ unique users, it rivals the Conference Room in sheer volume and surpasses it in the department of existential political argumentation.

APC-21 is the place where TLM members go to discuss things that probably shouldn't be discussed at work, including pandemics, constitutional law, GPU prices, and whether a given piece of technology is worth owning. It is, in the truest sense, a digital watercooler — the spot where conversations happen precisely because they should not, and where off-topic tangents are not merely tolerated but celebrated.

Overview

The name "APC-21" has no universally agreed-upon meaning within the server, which only adds to its mystique. The channel sits in the Watercooler Talk category, separating it from both the corporate channels above and the Scranton Branch channels where most social activity occurs.

APC-21 is notable for attracting some of the most passionate and sustained discussions in TLM history. Unlike the Conference Room, which leans toward casual banter, APC-21 has periodically hosted genuine intellectual combat — debates with numbered arguments, cited sources, and documented "deflection counts."

History and Major Eras

The Coronavirus Panic (Early 2020)

APC-21's most chaotic period occurred during the early months of the COVID-19 pandemic. The channel served as the primary venue for pandemic discourse, including:

  • Bunker preparation jokes — Members theorized about survival strategies with varying levels of sincerity.
  • Geographic humor — Missouri was identified as a location to be avoided, for reasons that remain unclear.
  • Area 51 vaccine conspiracy jokes — Predating the actual vaccine rollout by a year, members joked about experimental government serums.
  • Super Bowl LIV references — The Super Bowl was half-seriously identified as a potential disease vector / "ground zero."
  • Anti-HIV drug cocktail memes — The channel's medical knowledge was, charitably, creative.
"The coronavirus will kill everyone! Let's talk about it."
— Approximate spirit of the APC-21 tone, early 2020

See also: The Coronavirus Panic of 2020

The Constitutional Debate

At some point in APC-21's history, a discussion about states' rights versus federal authority erupted into one of the most formally structured arguments in server history. Key participants included members going by drcocoapuff, BigBoyEen (Ianternet McAnus), and LibriSlayer (Thaddy Thee Galleon).

The debate centered on the Supreme Court's ruling in Obergefell v. Hodges and the constitutional framework for federal authority over state law. The argument was notable for its unusual rigor — participants deployed numbered arguments and someone maintained an actual deflection count, treating the debate like a formal parliamentary proceeding.

The constitutional debate stands as APC-21's most intellectually intense moment and evidence that the server, when sufficiently motivated, can produce discourse indistinguishable from a college political science seminar.

See also: The Constitutional Debate

The Tech News Era

In more recent years, APC-21 has served as a primary hub for technology news sharing. Members — particularly I'll Bring the Beards — regularly post links via FXTwitter embeds covering:

  • GPU pricing and availability
  • AI and software developments
  • Social media platform changes
  • General internet culture commentary

Biggest Moments

The COVID Panic Thread

The extended COVID discussion of early 2020 was APC-21's most sustained single-topic event. The thread combined genuine concern with elaborate dark humor in a ratio that has never been precisely determined. It remains the fullest expression of the channel's capacity for collective anxiety management through jokes.

The Constitutional Standoff

The Obergefell debate elevated APC-21 briefly from a watercooler to a constitutional law seminar. The deflection count system was particularly novel — an attempt to hold participants accountable for avoiding direct engagement with arguments. It is unclear whether the count was ever officially settled.

Tech News Aggregation

The channel's transformation into a tech news hub has made it one of the more consistently informative channels in TLM. If you want to know what's happening in the world of GPUs, AI, or social media platforms, APC-21 is where to look.

Channel Culture

APC-21 has a distinct culture compared to other TLM channels. It is:

  • More intellectually aggressive — arguments here go deeper and last longer than in other channels.
  • More topically diverse — almost anything can appear in APC-21 at any time.
  • Less meme-dependent — while memes appear, APC-21 leans more toward text-based discussion than channels like Memememes.
  • Historically significant — the channel's archive contains some of the most important server conversations on record.
