Survivor Man is Threat Level Midnight's channel for firearms, outdoor recreation, and everyday carry (EDC) discussion. With 343 messages over three years, it is a relatively low-traffic channel — befitting a community where most members' survival skills are tested primarily by slow internet connections. Nonetheless, it has cultivated a dedicated core of outdoors enthusiasts led by the dual authorities of RudeLilSquarepants and Small Steve, who share exactly 68 messages each in what may be the most perfectly balanced rivalry in TLM history.

Overview

The channel covers a broad range of outdoors and tactical topics: gun purchases, range trips, concealed handgun permit (CHP) discussions, fishing aspirations, and the kind of gear talk that inevitably leads to someone posting their pocket contents on a white background. Ianternet (61 messages) provides regular contributions, while bUNGER (33) and Trenton (36 combined across aliases) round out the active roster.

Firearms Discourse

The channel's primary subject is firearms — purchases, recommendations, range etiquette, and the eternal question of what to carry. RudeLilSquarepants has emerged as the channel's most quotable firearms commentator:

"I keep that thing on me... Combat scissors"
— RudeLilSquarepants, redefining the concept of everyday carry
"also the mini14 is soooo frickin expensive"
— RudeLilSquarepants, confronting the economics of the Second Amendment

CHP discussions are a recurring theme, with multiple members at various stages of the permitting process. RudeLilSquarepants notably has a roommate who is a CHP instructor — a living arrangement that is either extremely convenient or extremely intimidating, depending on one's perspective.

"Most ranges won't let you conceal on the line"
— Ianternet, sharing range safety knowledge

The Chiappa Rhino Incident

Trenton's endorsement of the rainbow Chiappa Rhino — a revolver known for its unusual bottom-barrel design and, apparently, its availability in rainbow finish — stands as one of the channel's most memorable moments:

"the rainbow chiappa rhino goes hard"
— Trenton, advocating for tactical fabulousness

Beyond Firearms

Despite the channel's lean toward firearms content, Survivor Man also hosts discussion of fishing, camping, and general outdoor activities. bUNGER's aspiration to fish more represents the channel's gentler side:

"I want to fish some more this year"
— bUNGER, expressing a resolution shared by approximately 40% of American men annually

Cultural Significance

Survivor Man occupies a unique niche in TLM's channel ecosystem — it is the server's most overtly practical channel, dealing in real-world objects that can be held, fired, and occasionally used to catch dinner. In a server dominated by digital entertainment, it serves as a reminder that some hobbies still require leaving the house.
