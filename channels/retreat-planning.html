Retreat Planning (formally 🏕️-retreat-planning) is a restricted-access Scranton Branch channel of Threat Level Midnight dedicated to the logistics of the server's in-person cabin retreats. With only 52 messages, it is among TLM's smallest channels — a reflection not of its insignificance but of its operational security posture. This is the war room. Not everyone gets in.

"Only we can see this channel"
— Marshall, establishing the classified nature of retreat logistics

Overview

Retreat Planning exists as the organizational backbone of TLM's Cabin Retreat events — those rare occasions when Discord friends demonstrate that they are, in fact, willing to occupy the same physical space. The channel's restricted access ensures that retreat logistics remain in the hands of a select planning committee, preventing the chaos that would ensue if the entire server attempted to coordinate meal planning simultaneously.

"welcome to the planning committee channel"
— Marshall, granting access to TLM's inner circle

The Planning Committee

Marshall (18 messages) serves as de facto retreat coordinator, handling the organizational questions that keep a weekend gathering from descending into anarchy. Ben (14 messages) provides secondary logistical support, while Small Steve (10 messages) handles what can only be described as the retreat's most uniquely American contributions.

The Small Steve Arms Department

Small Steve's role in retreat planning centers on two key responsibilities: providing the cabin location and managing the ammunition supply chain. His messages reveal a man deeply committed to ensuring that every retreat attendee has access to adequate recreational firepower.

"Should I get enough extra so guys can buy some boxes to take home"
— Small Steve, on ammunition procurement
"should I buy some cheap 9mm to sell to the guys?"
— Small Steve, exploring a secondary market in bulk ammunition retail
"Show up around 5ish and bring some meat for yourself or to share. I will bring snacks if anybody is interested in shooting Blue Rock"
— Small Steve, combining hospitality with skeet shooting in a single sentence

Logistical Concerns

The channel's 52 messages are overwhelmingly practical, addressing the core questions of any gathering: when to arrive, what to eat, and who is bringing what.

"Bring your own meats might be fun"
— Ben, proposing the BYOM policy
"Breakfast in the morning? Should we all get together on some eggs and bacon"
— Marshall, planning the most important meal of the retreat
"She said free will donation to the Hope Venture"
— Ben, on the charitable component of the cabin arrangement

Activities

The retreat's planned activities, as documented in this channel, include:

  • Skeet/blue rock shooting — Facilitated by Small Steve's ammunition logistics
  • Meat grilling — BYOM (Bring Your Own Meats) format
  • Communal breakfast — Eggs and bacon, the universal language of morning bonding
  • Jackbox games — For when the group needs to return indoors
  • Pizza Fridays — Recurring "last Friday" pizza events within the broader community

Cultural Significance

Retreat Planning represents TLM at its most operationally serious. The channel contains no memes, no shitposting, and no philosophical tangents — just logistics. That a group of people who spend most of their time exchanging cursed images and arguing about fantasy football can produce 52 messages of pure, focused event planning is perhaps the greatest testament to what TLM is capable of when it commits to something.
