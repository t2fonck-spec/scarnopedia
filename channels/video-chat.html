Video Chat (formally 🎬-video-chat) is a Scranton Branch channel of Threat Level Midnight dedicated to video production, filmmaking, and the shared suffering of working with Adobe Premiere Pro. Active since March 2019, it is one of TLM's oldest channels and serves as the server's de facto film school, production studio, and post-production complaint department.

Overview

With 4,142 messages and approximately 13 MB of content spanning nearly seven years, Video Chat is among TLM's most substantial channels. The top three posters — Ben (1,298), Marshall (1,036), and Bill (950) — account for the vast majority of discussion, forming a triumvirate of filmmaking expertise that ranges from professional directing to enthusiastic experimentation.

The Three Pillars

Bill: The Director

Bill operates as TLM's closest connection to professional filmmaking, occasionally dropping dispatches from actual production sets with the casual energy of someone texting from a grocery store.

"Just wrapped directing a psa in Seattle! Woo-hoo!"
— Bill, announcing a professional directing credit the way most people announce finding a good parking spot
"a not so humble brag... working with some raw R3D (red camera) files in 4k on my new PC and seeing no lag"
— Bill, achieving the filmmaker's equivalent of enlightenment

Ben: The Editor

Ben leads the channel in message count and in documented frustration with editing software. His relationship with Adobe Premiere Pro is best described as adversarial — a years-long conflict in which neither side has achieved a decisive victory.

"I just added a vignette and it tanked my performance... Why is it that when you use premiere's features it doesn't work"
— Ben, asking the question that has united video editors since the dawn of NLE software
"Is this what going from film to digital felt like?"
— Ben, experiencing a technological paradigm shift

Marshall: The Creative

Marshall (King Scubdu) contributes through creative video projects of occasionally staggering file sizes, treating the Discord upload limit as a suggestion rather than a rule.

"work in progress"
— Marshall, attaching a 235 MB video file with the understated confidence of someone who has never heard of compression

Technical Discourse

Video Chat's discussions cover the full spectrum of production topics: camera selection, lens choices, lighting setups, color grading workflows, and the perennial debate over which codec will destroy your timeline the least. The channel has documented the evolution of consumer and prosumer video technology from 2019 through 2026, serving as an inadvertent time capsule of the industry's relentless march toward higher resolutions and larger file sizes.

Recurring topics include:

  • Camera discussions — RED, Blackmagic, and consumer cameras all receive analysis
  • Editing software wars — Premiere Pro vs. everything, with Premiere somehow always winning despite being universally despised
  • Render times — The channel's unofficial unit of time measurement
  • 4K workflows — The intersection of ambition and storage capacity

Supporting Cast

Thad (266 messages), Drew (195), Ian (125), and Goffers (80) round out the channel's contributor list, providing audience feedback, occasional creative input, and the moral support necessary to sustain a seven-year conversation about video codecs.

Cultural Significance

Video Chat is the channel that most directly connects TLM to creative output beyond the server itself. While most channels document what happens inside the Discord, Video Chat documents what TLM's members create in the outside world — and then discusses, at length, which export settings they should have used.
