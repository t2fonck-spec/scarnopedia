RL (formally 🚗-rl) is a gaming channel in Threat Level Midnight dedicated to Rocket League, the vehicular soccer game that has consumed Trenton's life since at least March 2023. With 863 messages spread across nearly three years, the channel maintains a slow but steady cadence — less a gaming hub and more a long-running diary of one man's quest to reach Grand Champion, with occasional interruptions from his friends.

Overview

Unlike the explosive, server-wide mobilization of the COD era, the RL channel represents a more intimate form of gaming community. The game's steep learning curve and emphasis on mechanical skill created a natural hierarchy: those who could aerial, and those who could not. Trenton, with 182 messages, sat firmly atop both the message leaderboard and the skill ladder, grinding ranks with a dedication that bordered on the vocational.

"ima try to grind up to getting gc rewards before the season is up"
— Trenton, describing what most people would call a second job

The Cool Ranch Incident

The channel's single most cited event occurred not in Rocket League itself but in its text chat, when Action Adam received a 72-hour ban for what can only be described as a Dorito-related opinion crime:

"i got a 72 hour ban from rocket league for calling a guy a basic bitch for saying cool ranch doritos were his favorite"
— Action Adam, a martyr for flavor discourse

The ban — three full days for expressing a position on corn chip seasoning — has become one of TLM's most frequently referenced anecdotes. Scholars remain divided on whether the punishment was disproportionate to the offense or whether Psyonix's moderation team simply has strong feelings about Cool Ranch. What is not in dispute is that Action Adam was, technically, correct: it is a basic choice.

The Bill Car Nation Proposal

Marshall's suggestion to name the TLM Rocket League team "Bill Car Nation" represents peak TLM naming convention — a pun that works on exactly zero levels but was proposed with complete sincerity. Whether the name was ever formally adopted is unclear; what matters is that it was proposed at all.

"Could also just call it bill car nation"
— Marshall (King Scubdu), workshopping team names

The MMR Question

Rocket League's matchmaking rating (MMR) system introduced a social tension absent from TLM's other gaming channels: the skill gap. Playing with lower-ranked friends meant risking one's carefully cultivated rating. Mox addressed this dilemma with characteristic directness:

"I'll torch my own MMR any time any where but don't want to torch yours unless I'm popping off"
— Mox, offering conditional self-sacrifice

The phrase "unless I'm popping off" does considerable heavy lifting here, transforming an act of generosity into a conditional contract. Ben, meanwhile, contributed to the channel's atmosphere of friendly antagonism:

"Don't forget to capitalize your sentences grammar boi"
— Ben (bUNGER), maintaining standards in a car soccer channel

Top Contributors

Trenton (182) led decisively, followed by Computron (157) — the bot tracking Rocket League YouTube updates with mechanical persistence — Ben (124), Marshall (59), Bill (58), Action Adam (44), and Thad (38). The Computron bot's second-place finish in yet another gaming channel suggests either an extremely prolific content pipeline or a bot that simply refuses to be outposted by humans.

Cultural Significance

RL is TLM's longest-running active gaming channel, spanning March 2023 through January 2026 with no sign of stopping. Its longevity is owed not to mass participation but to the devotion of a small core — primarily Trenton — who found in car soccer something worth grinding for nearly three years. In a server where most games burn bright and fast, Rocket League is the slow ember.
