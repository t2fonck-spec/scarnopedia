Our Dads is, with exactly 30 messages over four years, the smallest active channel in Threat Level Midnight. It is also, by virtually any measure, the most wholesome. The channel exists for a singular purpose: members share photographs of their fathers, celebrate Father's Day, and occasionally recommend dad-appropriate movies. There are no arguments. There are no controversies. There is only pure, uncut dad energy.

Overview

The channel's contributor list reads like a roll call of TLM's most sentimental members: King Scubdu leads with 9 messages, followed by Agent Michael Scarn and bUNGER at 7 each, I'll Bring the Beards with 3, and Trenton with 2. The remaining messages come from other members, including a notable contribution from Dinkin Flicka. At an average of roughly 7.5 messages per year, Our Dads operates on a geological timescale — erupting briefly around Father's Day before returning to dormancy.

The Dad Photo Initiative

The channel's core activity is the sharing of dad photographs, a tradition championed by bUNGER:

"Everyone needs to find pics of their dads"
— bUNGER, issuing a server-wide directive

Members have responded with varying degrees of enthusiasm, sharing photos that range from candid family snapshots to what can only be described as peak dad portraiture. The reactions to these photos are uniformly supportive:

"That is insanely glorious"
— Agent Michael Scarn, reacting to a dad photo with appropriate reverence
"love how clueless he looks in that photo"
— Dinkin Flicka, appreciating the universal dad aesthetic

Father's Day Traditions

The channel experiences its annual peak around Father's Day, when members gather to pay tribute to the men who raised them. Agent Michael Scarn has taken on the role of unofficial Father's Day announcer:

"Shoutout to the dads on this the day of fathers"
— Agent Michael Scarn, delivering a proclamation with the cadence of a medieval herald

Hairstyle Recommendations

At least one dad photo prompted unsolicited grooming advice:

"You should get some hair like that"
— I'll Bring the Beards, suggesting a member adopt their father's hairstyle

Cultural Significance

Our Dads is proof that even a Discord server built on shitposting and competitive Wordle has a heart. The channel's minuscule message count belies its outsized emotional significance — it is, in 30 messages, a portrait of a friend group that genuinely likes each other's families. In the TLM extended universe, there is no higher compliment than wanting to see someone's dad.
