Helldivers (formally 🪲-helldivers) is a gaming channel in Threat Level Midnight dedicated to Helldivers 2, the cooperative third-person shooter that became an instant cultural phenomenon upon its February 2024 launch. The channel is notable for being approximately 44% automated content from the APP bot, which relayed Central Command dispatches from the official Helldivers Discord — meaning that nearly half of the channel's "conversation" was propaganda from a fictional galactic military government. The members did not seem to mind.

Overview

Helldivers 2 arrived in TLM like a democratic airstrike: sudden, devastating, and accompanied by the sound of Marshall screaming about Star Wars. The game's premise — cooperative soldiers spreading "managed democracy" across the galaxy while fighting bugs and robots — resonated deeply with a server that had been searching for a post-Warzone identity. Within days of launch, the channel became a hub of tactical coordination, lore speculation, and enthusiastic readings of in-game propaganda dispatches.

The Central Command Pipeline

The APP bot's 621 messages — more than any human contributor — consisted primarily of forwarded dispatches from the official Helldivers Discord. These dispatches, written in the game's satirical military-propaganda style, were treated by TLM members with a degree of sincerity that the developers almost certainly did not intend. Marshall in particular engaged with these dispatches as though receiving actual intelligence briefings.

"many bothans died to bring us this information... the bots are building a death star... holy shit"
— Marshall (King Scubdu), seamlessly merging Star Wars and Helldivers lore

Marshall's tendency to process Helldivers events through a Star Wars lens extended to weaponry speculation:

"fuck lightsabers imagine running around with a giant fucking sword"
— Marshall, arriving at the correct conclusion about melee weapons

Notable Contributors

Behind the bot's wall of dispatches, the human hierarchy was led by Marshall (193), Drew (142), Ben (104), and Thad (94). Goffers contributed 70 messages, Josh 56, and Bill a modest 36 — his relative silence explained by a characteristic admission:

"Still need to play this game... Too busy rewatching breaking bad though"
— Bill (Agent Michael Scarn), prioritizing Heisenberg over Super Earth

Bill's refusal to engage with a game his entire friend group was playing because he was rewatching a show he had already seen remains one of the most Bill moments in the TLM archives.

The Sunday Diver Protocol

Thad established himself as the channel's most efficient time manager, completing his weekly homework during church services to free up Sunday afternoons for bug extermination:

"any sunday afternoon divers? I finished my whole week of homework during the sermon"
— Thad (Thaddy Thee Galleon), optimizing the salvation-to-gaming pipeline

Meanwhile, Bean Herbster contributed a rare but valuable life hack: joining someone else's party to skip matchmaking queues, a piece of tactical intelligence that outperformed most of the APP bot's dispatches in practical utility.

Cultural Significance

Helldivers represented TLM's first major collective gaming event since Warzone, though at 1,403 messages it achieved only about 12% of COD's volume. What it lacked in raw output it made up for in enthusiasm density — a metric that, while not formally defined, can be loosely understood as "Marshall messages per hour." The channel's sustained activity through September 2025 also made it one of TLM's longer-running gaming commitments, suggesting that managed democracy, unlike Verdansk, has staying power.
