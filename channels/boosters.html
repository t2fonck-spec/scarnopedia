Boosters (formally 🚀-boosters) is a server meta channel of Threat Level Midnight dedicated to the coordination — and confusion — of Discord Nitro server boosting. With only 15 messages, it is one of TLM's smallest channels by volume, yet it contains perhaps the highest concentration of bewilderment per message of any channel on the server.

Overview

The Boosters channel exists to answer a question that, based on the channel's contents, remains fundamentally unanswered: what does server boosting actually do? The channel's 15 messages span nearly a year and read less like a coordination effort and more like a support group for people who have accidentally spent money on something they do not understand.

"I honestly still don't understand boosting, what it does and how much we need to do it"
— Bill, speaking for the majority of Discord's user base

The Boost Economy

Marshall (5 messages) serves as the channel's boost economist, tracking the server's boost count with the intensity of a central banker monitoring interest rates. His analysis reveals a delicate ecosystem in which Level 2 represents the optimal target and Level 3 is considered an extravagance.

"I dropped my third one, so when that one goes away we'll have just enough for level 3. My only concern is level 2 but I appreciate Trenton's overspending"
— Marshall, performing boost calculus

The Trenton Overspend Incident

The channel's defining event is Trenton's accidental over-boosting of the server. In an act of generosity that exceeded both intention and comprehension, Trenton applied three boosts to the server — a quantity that even he found puzzling upon reflection.

"when I look at the boosts for the server it says 15 boosts?"
— Trenton, confused by the consequences of his own actions
"well I already did boost 2 obv"
— Thad, contributing to the boost count with characteristic nonchalance

Marshall's diplomatic acknowledgment of "Trenton's overspending" suggests a community that appreciates the gesture while gently questioning the financial reasoning behind triple-boosting a Discord server.

The Computron Incident

The Computron bot, which typically provides server utilities, apparently disables its commands in the Boosters channel. This policy prompted a characteristically direct response:

"fuck you"
— Ben, to Computron bot, upon discovering the command restriction

Participants

The channel's six contributors — Marshall (5), Ben (3), Computron (2), Thad (2), Trenton (2), and Bill (1) — represent TLM's boosting coalition. That Bill contributed exactly one message, and that message was an admission of total confusion, is a fitting encapsulation of the server boosting experience.

Cultural Significance

Boosters is a monument to the modern internet's ability to convince people to pay for things they cannot explain. The channel documents, in miniature, the entire lifecycle of a microtransaction economy: initial confusion, reluctant participation, accidental overspending, and the vague sense that someone should be keeping track of all this. That TLM achieved Level 3 boosting — a tier most servers aspire to — largely by accident is perhaps the most TLM outcome possible.
