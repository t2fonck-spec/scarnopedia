Stonks (formally 📈-stonks) is a Scranton Branch channel of Threat Level Midnight dedicated to financial discussion, stock market analysis, and cryptocurrency speculation. Founded in December 2020 — mere weeks before the GameStop short squeeze would reshape retail investing forever — Stonks arrived at the exact moment that every Discord server in existence suddenly needed a finance channel. Whether the timing was prophetic or coincidental remains a matter of scholarly debate.

Overview

With 1,772 messages across five years, Stonks operates at a leisurely pace of approximately one message per day — reflecting the channel's philosophy that good investments, like fine wine, should not be rushed. The channel serves as TLM's Wall Street trading floor, if Wall Street were staffed entirely by people who learned about options from Reddit.

The Bill Market

Bill (Agent Michael Scarn) leads the channel with 439 messages, establishing himself as TLM's foremost financial commentator and, more importantly, the server's most reliable contrarian indicator. Bill's investment strategy can be summarized as a single, remarkably consistent algorithm: buy at the all-time high.

"I once again bought some bitcoin when it was near an all-time high. Luckily this time I was smart enough to not buy enough to care if it goes to zero"
— Bill, achieving financial enlightenment through loss mitigation

Bill's crypto journey also included an ill-fated attempt to earn free Bitcoin through online surveys, which went about as well as one might expect:

"Even as I was typing it I felt like I was being hacked"
— Bill, on a free Bitcoin survey experience
"Eth about to hit 3500"
— Bill, demonstrating his signature blend of optimism and timing

The GameStop Era

Stonks launched in December 2020, positioning TLM perfectly for the January 2021 GameStop/AMC meme stock frenzy. The channel became a real-time commentary booth as members watched retail investors briefly terrify hedge funds. Drew (366 messages) and Thad (222 messages) contributed substantial analysis, while Small Steve provided measured takes that occasionally resembled actual financial advice.

"Not comparable GameStop was highly shorted I don't think there's nearly that much short action on alphabet"
— Small Steve, being the adult in the room
"hang in there @Agent Michael Scarn, you wanted to be a part of it, dont bail too soon!"
— Dinkin Flicka, offering the kind of encouragement that keeps portfolios red

Crypto Philosophy

The channel's crypto discussions range from earnest analysis to existential musings on the nature of currency itself. Thad, with a characteristic economy of words, once dismantled the entire fiat currency system in four words:

"just like the USD"
— Thad, responding to a criticism of cryptocurrency
"Arizona Ice Tea should be a stablecoin"
— Bill, proposing the only cryptocurrency backed by a beverage that has maintained its $0.99 price since 1992

Billcoin Crossover

Stonks shares thematic DNA with The Bill Chain, TLM's native cryptocurrency experiment. The overlap between members discussing real financial instruments and members trading Billcoin is nearly a perfect circle, suggesting that TLM's financial literacy pipeline runs directly from "what is a stock" to "I invented a currency."

Poster Hierarchy

The channel's posting distribution reveals a clear class structure: Bill (439), Drew (366), Thad (222), Ben (195), Ian (162), Dinkin Flicka (125), and Small Steve (122). Notably, Dinkin Flicka — a bot — outpaces several human members, raising questions about whether algorithmic trading has infiltrated TLM's discourse.

Cultural Significance

Stonks represents TLM's collective attempt to understand capitalism. That this attempt has been ongoing for five years with no clear resolution is perhaps the most authentic Wall Street experience the server could offer. The channel's greatest contribution to financial theory is the demonstration that buying at the all-time high, repeatedly, across multiple asset classes, is not a mistake but a lifestyle.
